Statistics of Death Rates From Air Pollution

Higher Death Rates: Long-term exposure to air pollution has been linked to increased mortality rates due to respiratory and cardiovascular diseases. According to the World Health Organization (WHO), air pollution contributes to millions of premature deaths worldwide each year. Especially in counties like china air pollutant is responsible for about 7 million deaths per year.

Hence The Importantance Of Maintaining Clean Air Quality

Reduces Respiratory Issues: When pollution levels are kept low, the risk of developing respiratory diseases such as asthma, bronchitis, and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) decreases. Cleaner air means fewer irritants that can inflame the airways and lungs. With better air quality it also reduces the change of children developing respiratory issues at a young age, since childrens organs are sitll developing it makes them more vulnerable to health risks from exposure to polluted air. Making it essential to take pecautions to protect their well being.

Better Cardiovascular Health: Studies show that lower air pollution levels are associated with a lower risk of heart attacks, strokes, and other cardiovascular diseases. Pollutants like fine particulate matter (PM2.5) can enter the bloodstream and cause inflammation, leading to heart problems.
